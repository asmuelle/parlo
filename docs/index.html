Why on-device

Why Parlo runs on your phone

Unlimited isn't a discount. It's where the cost went to zero.

Cloud tutors meter your speaking minutes because every spoken turn costs them money. Parlo runs the model on your phone, so each conversation costs nothing to run — which is exactly why we can let you talk forever, even with no signal at all.

  • Works with no connection

    You practice exactly where data dies — planes, subways, abroad without roaming. Parlo runs locally, so airplane mode is a feature, not a failure.

  • Truly unlimited turns

    No daily cap, no minute counter, no "you've used your practice for today." Stumble, repeat, and ramble as much as you need to actually get fluent.

  • Private by default

    Your voice is processed on your device, not streamed to a server. For shy learners, that means you can fumble a sentence without an audience — or a recording — anywhere but your own phone.

Straight answers

The honest questions.

Does it really work offline?

Yes. Speech recognition, the conversation model, and the spoken replies all run on your device — there's no cloud round-trip per turn. Switch on airplane mode and Parlo keeps holding a conversation, which is the whole point: you can practice on a flight, in a subway, or abroad with no data.

Which languages and phones will be supported at launch?

We're launching iPhone-first with a small set of high-quality languages — starting with Spanish, French, and German — on recent iPhones capable of running the on-device model. We'd rather ship a few languages that feel good than many that don't. More languages and Android support will follow as the on-device models and hardware allow.

Is Parlo as smart as a cloud tutor like ChatGPT?

We're honest about this: an on-device model is smaller than the giant cloud ones, so Parlo is built as a conversation-fluency partner for speaking volume — not a flawless grammar oracle. It can occasionally make mistakes, especially on fine nuance. Use it to build the confidence and reps that talking demands, and lean on a teacher or reference for the subtle stuff.

Why not just use a free cloud app?

If you always have signal and don't mind your voice going to a server, cloud apps are great. Parlo is for the times that aren't that: no connection, no data plan, or simply wanting unlimited, private practice without a meter. Those are the moments most learners actually have time to practice — and the ones cloud economics make expensive.
